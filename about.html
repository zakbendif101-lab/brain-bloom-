Why I Started Brain Bloom

I wanted to build the tutoring company
I'd actually trust with my own child.

When I started looking at how other tutoring companies operated, the same patterns kept showing up: oversized groups, high tutor turnover, generic worksheets, and very little follow-through.

I knew I could do it differently. Smaller groups — five children, never more. A method that builds understanding, not just answers. Specialist teaching from people who actually know their subject. And a guarantee — because if you can't stand behind the work, you shouldn't charge for it.

That's Brain Bloom. Every programme is built that way, from 11+ prep to the holiday camp, and I'm there for every single class.

My Method

The 3 C's: Confidence, Clarity, Consistency.

Three principles that sound simple. Done properly, they change how children learn.

C

Confidence

Belief precedes ability. Every session I teach opens with a win — a problem the child can solve — to ground them in capability before stretching them.

C

Clarity

If a child can teach a concept back in their own words, they truly own it. The Feynman Technique sits at the centre of how I explain, model and check understanding.

C

Consistency

Mastery comes from regular, focused practice — not last-minute cramming. I build study habits with my students that outlast the term.

My Guarantee

One level of progress
— or your money back.

If a child completes a full term of Brain Bloom tuition and hasn't moved up at least one level on standardised assessment, I refund. In full.

I can offer this because of how rarely it happens. Small groups, mastery-based curriculum and consistent attendance produce results — and I'd rather hold myself to that bar than dress it up in marketing language.

What "one level" means

I assess at the start of every term using standardised diagnostics. I assess again at the end. If your child hasn't progressed a full level — measured objectively, not by me — I refund. No paperwork. No conditions.

What I Hold To

Four principles that don't move.

Small groups, always

Five children maximum in weekly tuition. Anything bigger and the maths of attention stops working. I won't compromise on this — even when it would be commercially easier to.

I teach personally

You're booking me, not a tutor I've assigned. I lead every class personally — no agency staff, no rotating tutors, no surprises.

Honest before nice

If a programme isn't right for your child, I'll tell you. If your child needs more time on something, I'll tell you. The "nice" thing isn't always the right thing.

Two subjects, done brilliantly

English and Maths. I don't pretend to be a Science specialist or a language tutor. Depth over breadth, every time.
